
# Demo Scripts

Last Updated: 2026-05-18

## Purpose

This file defines the contractor-facing demo narrative for VOM.

The purpose of the demo is to:
- create belief
- demonstrate operational value
- reduce perceived risk
- show office replacement capability

This is not a feature dump.

The demo should feel:
- clear
- believable
- stable
- outcome-focused

## Demo Order

Step 1: Set the frame
Step 2: Trigger the demo call
Step 3: Show the booking outcome
Step 4: Show the dashboard activity
Step 5: Show onboarding as the next step
Step 6: Show invoice follow up as future proof
Step 7: Close the narrative

---

## Step 1: Set the Frame

Talking point:
"Right now your phone rings when you are on a job, driving, or with a crew.
If nobody answers, that lead is gone.
VOM answers every call, qualifies the caller, and books the appointment.
No missed calls. No missed revenue.
Let me show you exactly how it works."

Purpose:
Anchor the prospect in their own pain before the demo starts.
Do not mention features yet.

---

## Step 2: Trigger the Demo Call

Action:
Launch the demo trigger.
The Vapi voice agent answers as the Virtual Office Manager for the contractor business.

Talking point:
"This is what your customer hears when they call.
The agent handles the conversation, captures what they need, and moves toward booking."

Notes:
- Keep this live and visible
- Do not narrate over the agent
- Let the prospect hear the interaction
- If audio fails, have a fallback recording ready

Fallback script if live call fails:
"Let me show you the outcome the system creates after that interaction."
Move immediately to Step 3 without dwelling on the failure.

---

## Step 3: Show the Booking Outcome

Action:
Show the confirmed appointment created by the demo harness.

Talking point:
"The system captured the intent and created a confirmed appointment.
That appointment is now visible in the dashboard, tied to the customer record, and ready for your crew."

Notes:
- The booking outcome is deterministic for demo reliability
- If prospect asks whether the AI booked it in real time, answer honestly:
  "This is a controlled demo flow. In production the booking intent flows into a confirmation step before it locks in."
- Do not oversell real time booking if it is not yet live

---

## Step 4: Show the Dashboard Activity

Action:
Show the dashboard with call activity, booking status, and appointment details.

Talking point:
"This is your operations view.
Every call handled, every appointment booked, every customer interaction logged.
You are not chasing your phone anymore.
The system shows you what happened and what is next."

Notes:
- Point to the activity timeline
- Show the appointment card prominently
- Keep this visual and brief
- Do not walk through every metric

---

## Step 5: Show Onboarding as the Next Step

Action:
Show the onboarding screen.

Talking point:
"When you bring on a new contractor client, this is where they get set up.
Business hours, service area, intake routing.
The system learns the business and handles calls accordingly."

Notes:
- This is a placeholder demonstration
- Do not walk through every field
- The goal is to show the system is built for real business setup
- One sentence is enough: "This is where your business profile lives."

---

## Step 6: Show Invoice Follow Up as Future Proof

Action:
Show the invoice tracking view and follow up status.

Talking point:
"Once a job is done, VOM tracks the invoice.
When a payment is overdue, the system prepares a follow up.
No more chasing customers manually.
The system handles it and flags anything that needs your attention."

Notes:
- This is a future phase demonstration
- Do not promise full automation is live today
- Frame it as: "This is where the system is heading and it is already built into the product."

---

## Step 7: Close the Narrative

Talking point:
"What you just saw is a virtual office manager handling the call, booking the job, logging the activity, and preparing for follow up.
No receptionist. No missed calls. No chasing invoices.
The question is how much revenue you are leaving on the table every week from calls that do not get answered."

Purpose:
Return to pain. Do not summarize features.
End on a question that opens the next conversation.

## Objection Handling

Objection: Is this real or just a demo?
Response:
"What you saw is a controlled demo built on the real system.
The call handling is live. The booking flow in production includes a confirmation step before it locks in.
Everything you saw reflects how the system actually works."

Objection: What happens if the AI says the wrong thing?
Response:
"The agent follows a defined script for your business.
You approve the script before it goes live.
Anything outside that script gets flagged for your review."

Objection: What does this cost?
Response:
"That depends on the setup that fits your volume.
What I want to make sure first is that this actually solves the problem for you.
Does what you saw match what you are dealing with on your end?"

Objection: We already have someone who answers the phone.
Response:
"That is good. VOM is not here to replace someone you trust.
It handles the overflow, the after hours calls, and the follow up work that usually falls through the cracks.
Most contractors find those are the calls costing them the most."

Objection: I need to think about it.
Response:
"That makes sense. What part felt unclear or uncertain?
I want to make sure you have what you need to make the right call."

## Demo Rules

- Never read features off a list
- Always anchor to contractor pain first
- Keep the demo under 15 minutes
- Let silence work after the booking outcome
- Never apologize for the product
- If something breaks, move forward without dwelling
- One question to close, not a pitch
